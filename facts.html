Did you know?

1. Penguins don't have teeth.

Instead, these birds use their beak to grab their food. To help, they also have spines on their tongue and the top of their beak!

2. Fish makes up the majority of their diet.

Penguins also eat krill, shrimp, and crabs. Excessive fishing can be extremely dangerous for them. Many of them choose a specific type of fish and when the fish is no longer available or very small they starve themselves.

3. Penguins lose their feathers once a year!

A new waterproof coat grows, but until it fully grows, they are not allowed in the water. This process can take up to a few weeks at times. Most lose about half their body weight during this period of time.

4. A penguin's body temperature is 100 degrees Farenheit!

This high temperature allows penguins to live in colder temperatures and survive. Human body temperature is 97.9 to 99 degrees Farenheit for comparison.

5. They can stay underwater for 10-15 minutes!

Although these birds cannot fly in the air. Penguins can "fly" underwater! Some species swimming more than 20 mph!

6. The Gentoo penguin can swim up to 22 mph!

Most penguins can swim 5-6 mph, some with 15 mph bursts! Walking is about 1.7 to 2.4 mph for them. The fastest human ran about 24.81 mph for a 100 meters!

7. Typically penguins are not afraid of humans!

However, human interference is a problem. Interacting with their habitat. Being fed by tourists can also affect their metabolism and can be detramental to their health.

8. Most penguins are found in South Africa, New Zealand, Antartica, Argentina and Australia!

There are about 18 different species of penguins all ranging from the smallest of only two pounds and 10 to 12 inches tall, the blue penguin, to the Emperor penguin of average heights of 45 inches and can weigh up to 88 pounds!

9. The most rare penguin is called the Yellow Penguin.

There are about 5,000 living in New Zealand. They have yellow cat-like eyes.

10. Penguins are very social animals!

They swim and feeed in groups. A group of penguins is called a Colony! In Antartica there are some colonies with 20 million penguins!
